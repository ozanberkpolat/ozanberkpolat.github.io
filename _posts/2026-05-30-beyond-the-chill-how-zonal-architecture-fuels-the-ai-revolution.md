As AI workloads explode, the traditional datacenter is hitting a thermal wall. Managing the massive heat generated by next-gen GPUs alongside legacy CPUs requires more than just "cranking up the AC"—it requires a fundamental architectural shift. 

Microsoft is pioneering **Zonal Cooling**, a strategic design that moves away from inefficient, "one-size-fits-all" facility water systems. By utilizing independent cooling loops tailored for air and liquid-cooled hardware, these next-generation facilities can achieve an estimated **10% reduction in PUE** while maximizing chip performance. This modular approach allows for higher server density and the thermal flexibility needed for future AI accelerators, all while supporting Microsoft's aggressive commitment to *carbon-negative* and *water-positive* operations.
