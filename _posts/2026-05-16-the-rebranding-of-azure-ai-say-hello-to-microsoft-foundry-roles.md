Identity and Access Management (IAM) is the bedrock of secure cloud operations. If you have been overseeing Azure AI deployments, take note: a significant branding shift is currently rolling out. To better align with the evolving product ecosystem, Microsoft has officially updated its built-in role names to reflect the **Microsoft Foundry** brand.

> "Clarity in role naming is essential for scaling AI governance and ensuring seamless team collaboration."

This update streamlines the vocabulary for administrators and developers alike. Key transitions include renaming the *Azure AI Account Owner*, *Azure AI Owner*, and *Azure AI User* to their new **Foundry** counterparts. While the underlying permissions and technical capabilities remain consistent, these nomenclature changes are critical for keeping your internal documentation, audit logs, and IAM policies accurate. Staying ahead of these shifts ensures your team navigates the **Microsoft Foundry** landscape with precision.
